# AI Assistant

The AI Assistant is a conversational interface built into the tedge.ai platform. It lets you ask questions about the current market, your recent analyses, open positions, and general trading concepts — and it responds with context-aware answers that reference the actual data on your screen.


## How to Use It

Open the **Assistant** tab in the right sidebar (desktop) or bottom tabs (mobile). Type your question in the text field and press Enter. The AI responds in real time, streaming its answer as it generates it.

The assistant is designed for quick, conversational exchanges. Ask a question, get an answer, and follow up if you need more detail. There is no need to provide context about the current symbol or your recent analysis — the assistant already knows.

## What the Assistant Knows

The assistant has access to several layers of context that make its responses specific and relevant to your situation:

**Current symbol and timeframe** — It knows what chart you are looking at and can reference the current price, recent candles, and visible structure.

**Latest AI analysis** — If you have run an analysis on the current symbol, the assistant can reference the identified zones, opportunities, and the agent's reasoning. You can ask it to explain a specific zone or compare opportunities.

**Open positions** — The assistant knows about your currently open trades, including entry prices, P&L, SL/TP levels, and duration. Ask it whether you should hold or close, and it can provide context-aware guidance.

**Trade history** — Your past trades and AI Coach reviews are part of the assistant's context. Ask it about patterns in your trading or for a summary of recent performance.

**Trading profile** — Your risk preferences, trading style, and favorite instruments shape the assistant's recommendations to match your approach.

## Example Questions

Here are some practical ways to use the assistant:

- "What's the current trend on BTC?" — Gets a structural assessment based on the visible chart.
- "Should I take this long setup?" — Evaluates the current opportunity against the analysis context.
- "Explain the order block at 67000" — Provides detail about a specific zone from the analysis.
- "What's my risk if I enter here with a SL at 65500?" — Calculates position sizing and dollar risk.
- "Compare the H1 and H4 bias" — Contrasts the structural picture across timeframes.
- "How have my BTC trades performed this month?" — Summarizes your trading history for that symbol.
- "What mistakes am I making most often?" — Analyzes your AI Coach reviews for recurring patterns.

> **Tip:** The assistant works best when you ask specific, focused questions. Instead of "What should I trade?", try "Is there a good entry opportunity on EUR/USD based on my last analysis?"

## When to Use It

The assistant is most valuable in three scenarios:

**During analysis review** — After running an AI analysis, use the assistant to clarify zones, understand the reasoning, or explore alternative interpretations of the market structure.

**Before executing a trade** — Ask the assistant to evaluate the setup, check for confluences you might have missed, or calculate your risk for a specific entry.

**After a trade** — Discuss the AI Coach review, ask for elaboration on specific feedback points, or explore what you could do differently next time.


## Credit Cost

Each message sent to the assistant consumes approximately 0.03 AI credits. This is significantly less than running a full analysis, making the assistant suitable for frequent, casual use. The assistant is available on Basic plans and above.

## Limitations

The assistant does not execute trades or modify positions on your behalf. It provides analysis, guidance, and information — but all trading actions must be taken through the platform's trading interface. The assistant also does not have access to external news feeds or fundamental data; its context is limited to what is available within the tedge.ai platform.

> **Warning:** The AI Assistant provides analysis and educational content, not financial advice. Always use your own judgment when making trading decisions. Past performance of AI-suggested setups does not guarantee future results.
