# Trade Journal

The Trade Journal lives on the Analyses page as an integrated panel, connecting your trades directly to the analyses that generated them. It provides a structured space for reviewing trades, adding personal notes, and replaying historical setups to reinforce your learning.


## Accessing the Journal

Navigate to the **Analyses** page and open the Journal panel from the sidebar. The journal displays trade cards organized chronologically, each linked to the analysis that produced the corresponding opportunity.

## Trade Cards

Each card in the journal represents a closed trade and includes:

- **Symbol and direction** — What you traded and in which direction.
- **Entry and exit prices** — Where you got in and where you got out.
- **P&L and R:R** — The financial result and risk-to-reward ratio achieved.
- **AI Coach score** — The execution quality rating from the AI Coach.
- **Your notes** — Personal observations, thoughts, and lessons you have written about the trade.
- **Replay button** — Click to jump to the analysis replay for that trade, with the chart positioned at the trade's time window.

## Adding and Editing Notes

Click on any trade card to expand it and access the notes editor. Write your thoughts about the trade in free-form text — what you were thinking when you entered, what you observed during the trade, and what you would do differently next time.

Notes are saved automatically as you type. They persist alongside the AI Coach review, giving you both an objective machine assessment and your own subjective perspective on each trade.

> **Tip:** Writing notes consistently is one of the most effective ways to improve as a trader. The act of articulating your reasoning forces you to think critically about your decisions, and reviewing old notes reveals patterns you might not notice otherwise.

## Replaying from the Journal

Each trade card includes a **Replay** button that opens the analysis replay view, positioned at the time the trade was active. You see the chart with the AI's original zones, your entry and exit markers, and the replay controls to step through price action candle by candle.

This makes the journal a powerful study tool — pick a trade from your history, read your notes and the AI Coach review, then replay the chart to see exactly what happened and why.


## Filtering

The journal panel provides filters to help you find specific trades:

- **By symbol** — Show only trades for a specific instrument.
- **By date range** — Focus on a particular period.
- **By result** — Show only winning or losing trades.
- **By AI Score** — Filter by execution quality rating.

## Stats Summary

At the top of the journal panel, a summary bar displays key statistics for the currently filtered set of trades:

- Total trades and win rate.
- Average P&L and total P&L.
- Average AI Coach score.
- Most traded symbol.

These stats update dynamically as you apply filters, making it easy to analyze your performance across different instruments, time periods, or quality ranges.

## Journal vs Trade History

The journal and the Trade History page show the same trades but serve different purposes. The Trade History page is a data-driven performance dashboard with tables, charts, and statistics. The journal is a reflective workspace that connects trades to their analyses and your personal notes, designed for learning and review.

> **Tip:** Use the Trade History page for quantitative analysis (win rate trends, equity curve, calendar patterns) and the journal for qualitative review (why did I take this trade? what did I learn? what would I do differently?).
